The situation

I designed a 16-page typographic specimen that showcased the unique features, uses, and background of the typeface IBM Plex Serif. Since the project was for my typographic design class, one of the constraints I had to follow was that I was not allowed to use images in my design. I used type handling, layout, and color to create a unique design that showcased the versatility and unique features of IBM Plex Serif.

Why IBM Plex Serif?

Over the summer, I learned about IBM's new corporate typeface superfamily, IBM Plex, as a participant in the design track of the IBM Accelerate Program. I chose the serif variation because it was intriguing how it had influences from older typographic styles yet still reflected IBM as a modern technology company.

Research and content

Before starting on the design, I had to do background research on IBM Plex Serif in order to write the content contained in my type specimen. This proved to be a little challenging since the typeface is fairly new, so there wasn't a ton of information on it compared to older typefaces.

The design process

After I designed my first draft, my professor commented that the design reflected IBM because it was pretty boring. While I disagreed with her assessment of IBM being a boring company (the IBM Accelerate Program showed me otherwise), I agreed that my first draft was pretty boring. The critique from my professor and my classmates helped me in designing my future drafts to better reflect how interesting IBM and its typeface actually are.

Focusing on a final design

One thing that helped me improve my design was to focus on a key message. IBM describes the IBM Plex family as reflecting the company's mission of connecting ‚Äúmankind and machine.‚Äù I noticed specific features of the typeface that reflected that value, so I leaned into that with my design.

Design decisions

  • I used large punctuation marks as dominant elements on some pages to create visual interest and draw the eye into specific parts of the layout. This also helped to showcase IBM Plex Serif's unique punctuation marks.
  • Some visual elements and motifs I repeated throughout my design, such as the blue lines (representative of the bars in IBM's logo). These lines helped to bring cohesiveness to the design of my type specimen, while also serving specific purposes in their individual usage.
