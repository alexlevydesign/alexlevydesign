CuseHacks Branding

Roles

Designer • Content writer • Social media manager

Tools

Adobe Illustrator

Outcome

I created a new brand for a student-run hackathon, designing a new wordmark, posters, signage, stickers, and social media posts. My branding and social media campaign helped grow the CuseHacks Instagram follower count by 4.5%. We also drew a sizable attendance of more than 50 people.

View final design

The situation

As a designer for CuseHacks (a student-run hackathon at Syracuse University), I was tasked with designing a new brand for the Spring 2023 hackathon based on a theme, which for that event was gaming.

The design process

Brand-wide design decisions

Typography

  • I chose a video game themed display typeface for top-level headings and the wordmark, but used a more versatile sans-serif typeface for everything else.
  • I often had to adjust the kerning on the display typeface when the spacing between letters looked uneven.

Pattern

  • I made a pattern based on glowing green lines that look like glowing wires to emphasize the technical basis of the hackathon while also slightly hinting at the gaming theme.
  • This visual element served a functional purpose as well as an aesthetic one. Since the lines contrast so much with the background, I used this pattern to draw attention to certain elements by pointing at them or boxing them in.

Colors

  • I chose orange and green because the orange associated the event with Syracuse University, and the green added a technical, retro feel.
  • I designed all branded materials with a black background to make the green wire pattern pop out from the background. If I had made the background white, there wouldn't have been enough contrast

Outcome

My branding and social media campaign helped grow the CuseHacks Instagram follower count by 4.5%. We also drew a sizable attendance of over 50 people.
